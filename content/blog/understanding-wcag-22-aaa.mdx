## Why AAA Matters

Most organizations aim for WCAG AA compliance — and that's a solid baseline. But AAA takes accessibility further, ensuring your digital products are usable by the widest possible audience, including users with severe visual, auditory, cognitive, and motor impairments.

After managing web accessibility for over 5,000 pages at Bank of America, I've seen firsthand how AAA compliance doesn't just help users with disabilities — it improves the experience for everyone.

## Key AAA Requirements

### Enhanced Contrast Ratios

While AA requires a 4.5:1 contrast ratio for normal text, AAA raises the bar to **7:1**. This means your color palette needs careful consideration from the start.

### No Background Audio

AAA requires that any background audio can be turned off, is very quiet, or is absent entirely. This goes beyond AA's requirement for captions.

### Sign Language Interpretation

For prerecorded audio content, AAA requires sign language interpretation — a significant commitment but one that opens your content to the Deaf community.

## Practical Implementation

Building for AAA from day one is far easier than retrofitting. Here's the approach we take at Accessible Computing:

1. **Design system first** — Establish AAA-compliant color tokens before writing a single component
2. **Semantic HTML** — Use proper heading hierarchy, landmarks, and ARIA roles
3. **Keyboard navigation** — Every interactive element must be reachable and operable via keyboard
4. **Focus management** — Visible, consistent focus indicators with sufficient contrast
5. **Testing throughout** — Automated tools catch ~30% of issues; manual testing catches the rest

## The Business Case

Accessibility isn't just compliance — it's a competitive advantage. Accessible websites rank better in search engines, reach more customers, and reduce legal risk. The ROI is clear.
