## The Challenge

Bank of America's digital presence spanned thousands of customer-facing pages across multiple business lines. Ensuring consistent accessibility compliance at this scale required a systematic, enterprise-wide approach.

## The Approach

As VP and Web Accessibility Team Manager, I led the initiative to establish and enforce accessibility standards across the organization:

### Standards & Governance

- Developed internal accessibility standards based on WCAG 1.0 and Section 508
- Created review processes for all new and modified customer-facing pages
- Established accessibility checkpoints within the development lifecycle

### Scale & Execution

- Managed compliance for **5,000+ pages** across multiple business units
- Trained development teams on accessibility best practices
- Built automated testing pipelines for continuous compliance monitoring

### Cross-Functional Leadership

- Worked across marketing, legal, technology, and business teams
- Balanced compliance requirements with business objectives
- Maintained standards during rapid feature development cycles

## Key Results

- Enterprise-wide accessibility compliance achieved and maintained
- Reduced accessibility defects by standardizing development practices
- Built internal expertise that sustained beyond the initial initiative
- Established patterns later adopted across the industry

## Lessons Learned

1. **Top-down commitment is essential** — Accessibility needs executive sponsorship to succeed at scale
2. **Automation helps but isn't enough** — Automated tools catch roughly 30% of accessibility issues
3. **Training scales better than auditing** — Teach developers to build accessible from the start
4. **Standards evolve** — What was compliant in 2004 may not meet today's requirements
